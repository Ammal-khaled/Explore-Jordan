Top Destinations

An Ancient Masterpiece Petra:

Carved from rose-red sandstone, this UNESCO World Heritage site awakens the senses and transports you back in time.

Valley of the Moon Wadi Rum:

Vast desert landscapes and traditional Bedouin camps offer a serene adventure in the desert.

Beneath the Waves of Aqaba:

Plunge into a turquoise wonderland, where the underwater world of Aqaba beckons with its vibrant marine life and kaleidoscope of colors.

The Capital City Amman:

A vibrant mix of ancient ruins and modern life bustling with culture and cuisine.

The Healing Waters Dead Sea:

Float effortlessly at the Earth's lowest point, famous for its high salinity and therapeutic mud.

The Roman City of Jerash:

Walk through one of the best preserved Roman provincial towns in the world with its colonnaded streets and ancient theaters.

Dana Biosphere Reserve:

Jordan's largest nature reserve with stunning mountain scenery and diverse wildlife across four different bio-geographical zones.

The Mosaic City of Madaba:

Famous for its spectacular Byzantine and Umayyad mosaics, especially the 6th century map of Jerusalem and Holy Land.

Ajloun Castle:

A 12th century Islamic castle built atop Mount 'Auf with panoramic views of the Jordan Valley.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

Spring (Mar-May) and Autumn (Sep-Nov) offer mild weather and vivid views.

Cuisine

Must-tries include Mansaf (national dish), falafel, hummus, and Kanafeh for dessert.

Etiquette

Respect local customs, dress modestly especially in religious sites, accept tea/coffee when offered.

Currency

Jordanian Dinar (JOD). Mid-range budget: approx. $75–$150 USD per day.
