Jordan: Heritage, Nature, and Hospitality

Discover a country shaped by Nabataean ingenuity, Roman engineering, Islamic heritage, and Bedouin hospitality.

From Petra and Jerash to Wadi Rum and the Dead Sea, every journey reveals a different side of the kingdom.

Travel with curiosity, take time for tea and conversation, and experience the generosity that makes Jordan feel personal.

Top Destinations

Petra: The Rose-Red City

Walk through the Siq to Petra, where monumental Nabataean facades are carved directly into rose-colored sandstone. It is Jordan's most iconic archaeological treasure and a powerful introduction to the country's layered history.

Wadi Rum: Valley of the Moon

Wadi Rum is a protected desert of sandstone mountains, red dunes, and wide-open skies. Stay with local Bedouin hosts, explore by jeep or camel, and watch the landscape shift from gold to deep red at sunset.

Aqaba: The Red Sea Coast

Aqaba brings Jordan to the Red Sea, with warm beaches, coral reefs, and easy access to snorkeling and diving. It is a relaxed coastal stop after desert and heritage routes.

Amman: Ancient Meets Modern

Amman blends hilltop ruins, lively markets, galleries, cafes, and some of Jordan's best food. The city is an ideal base for understanding everyday Jordanian life.

Dead Sea: Mineral-Rich Waters

Float effortlessly in the mineral-rich waters of the Dead Sea, the lowest point on Earth. The shoreline is known for dramatic views, spa resorts, and restorative mud treatments.

Jerash: Roman Streets and Theaters

Walk among colonnaded streets, plazas, temples, and theaters in one of the best-preserved Roman cities in the region. Jerash is especially rewarding for travelers interested in archaeology and classical architecture.

Dana Biosphere Reserve

Jordan's largest nature reserve offers dramatic valleys, mountain trails, and quiet village landscapes. Dana is ideal for hikers, nature lovers, and travelers looking for a slower rural stay.

Madaba: The Mosaic City

Madaba is known for its detailed Byzantine and Umayyad mosaics, including the famous sixth-century map of the Holy Land. Its churches, workshops, and easygoing streets make it a rewarding cultural stop.

Ajloun Castle

Ajloun Castle is a 12th-century hilltop fortress built to guard regional trade routes. Its towers and viewpoints offer sweeping scenes of olive groves, forests, and the Jordan Valley.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

Spring and autumn bring comfortable temperatures, clear light, and excellent conditions for walking, sightseeing, and desert stays.

Cuisine

Try mansaf, maqluba, mezze, fresh falafel, local olive oil, and kanafeh for a sweet finish.

Etiquette

Dress modestly at religious and rural sites, greet people politely, and accept tea or coffee as a sign of hospitality when offered.

Currency

The Jordanian dinar (JOD) is the local currency. Cards are common in cities, while cash is useful for taxis, markets, and rural areas.

Accommodation Spotlight

Luxury Stays

Stay in polished city hotels, Dead Sea resorts, and Red Sea properties with strong service, comfortable rooms, and easy access to major sights.

Eco Lodges

Choose eco lodges near Dana and other nature areas for quiet landscapes, local meals, and a lower-impact way to experience Jordan's outdoors.

Desert Camps

Sleep in Wadi Rum desert camps where traditional hospitality, night skies, and simple comforts make the desert feel memorable and welcoming.
