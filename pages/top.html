Top Destinations in Jordan

Explore Jordan's signature destinations, from archaeological landmarks to desert reserves and coastal escapes.

Outdoor Historical Adventure

Petra

Petra is Jordan's most celebrated archaeological site, carved into rose-colored sandstone by the Nabataeans. Walk through the Siq to reach the Treasury, then continue to tombs, temples, and viewpoints that reveal the scale of the ancient city.

Outdoor Natural Adventure

Wadi Rum

Wadi Rum is a protected desert of red dunes, narrow canyons, and towering sandstone formations. Local Bedouin guides lead jeep rides, camel routes, and overnight stays beneath exceptionally clear skies.

View Map Details
Outdoor Natural

Dead Sea

The Dead Sea is famous for effortless floating, mineral-rich mud, and quiet resort beaches. Its shoreline also offers striking views across the water and a peaceful contrast to Jordan's busier heritage sites.

View Map Details
Outdoor Historical Cultural

Jerash

Jerash preserves one of the region's most complete Roman cityscapes, with colonnaded streets, plazas, temples, and theaters. It is an essential stop for understanding Jordan's classical history beyond Petra.

View Map Details
Outdoor Urban Adventure

Aqaba

Aqaba is Jordan's Red Sea gateway, known for coral reefs, warm beaches, and relaxed waterfront dining. It pairs easily with Wadi Rum for travelers who want both desert adventure and coastal downtime.

View Map Details
Outdoor Historical Urban

Amman Citadel

The Amman Citadel sits above the capital with ruins from Roman, Byzantine, and Umayyad periods. Its hilltop setting offers a clear view of the city's layered history and modern neighborhoods.

View Map Details
Outdoor Religious Historical

Mount Nebo

Mount Nebo is a revered viewpoint associated with Moses and the Promised Land. Visitors come for the sweeping views, peaceful church setting, and preserved mosaics that connect faith and history.

View Map Details
Indoor Cultural Historical

Jordan Museum

The Jordan Museum presents the country's story from prehistoric settlements to modern national identity. Its collections give useful context before or after visiting sites such as Petra, Jerash, and the Dead Sea region.

View Map Details
Outdoor Natural

Ma'in Hot Springs

Ma'in Hot Springs is known for warm mineral waterfalls tucked into a dramatic mountain valley. It is a restorative stop for travelers seeking quiet scenery and a slower pace.

Jordan's Vibrant Cities

Discover cities where historic streets, local markets, food culture, and modern neighborhoods sit side by side.

Amman

Amman is a welcoming capital of hills, Roman ruins, markets, galleries, and neighborhood cafes. It is also one of the best places to taste Jordanian food and see daily life unfold.

View Map Details

Aqaba

Aqaba offers Red Sea beaches, coral reefs, boat trips, and a laid-back waterfront atmosphere. Its location makes it a natural stop between Wadi Rum and southern Jordan's coastal experiences.

View Map Details

As-Salt

As-Salt is known for honey-colored Ottoman-era architecture, hillside streets, and a strong sense of community heritage. Its historic core offers a quieter, more local city experience.

View Map Details

Madaba

Madaba is known as the City of Mosaics, with churches and workshops preserving detailed Byzantine and Umayyad designs. The famous sixth-century map of the Holy Land remains one of its most important treasures.

View Map Details

Irbid

Irbid is a northern city with a youthful university atmosphere and easy access to archaeological sites such as Umm Qais. It is a useful base for exploring Jordan's green highlands and northern heritage routes.

View Map Details

Zarqa

Zarqa is one of Jordan's largest urban centers and a practical gateway to the eastern desert. Its surrounding region connects travelers with desert castles, open landscapes, and lesser-known routes beyond the capital.

View Map Details
